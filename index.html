    • Are there any other efficient ways that we can add to solar?
      • We could add hydropower, geothermal or nuclear, but geothermal and nuclear are not the most feasible, so we could do some form of hydropower, probably not a full scale hydropower plant, along with solar. Our factory will be in Phoenix, Arizona because it gets a lot of sunlight, and there is the Salt River that we could build a dam in and that could be the source of hydropower, and we would probably do the small type of hydropower.
    • In how many different ways can we hold energy in a self sustaining factory?
      • We can hold energy is self sustaining factories in many different ways. For example, we can have solar panels so that we can use resources really well and we can use the sun to power the factory. Apart from this, if we built a solar farm, that could get a lot of energy and there could still be things going on below the solar panels, like parking. Also, small hydropower is an option.
    • What are some energy storage techniques that are used widely?
      • Some energy storage techniques that are used widely include solid state batteries, which are electrochemical batteries that include advanced chemistry batteries and capacitors, flow batteries, where the energy is stored directly into the electrolyte solution, compressed air energy storage, where you use compressed air to store energy, thermal, where you capture the heat and cold to acquire energy on demand, and pumped hydro-power, where you rely on gravity causing the water to cascade, creating an on-demand energy source that can be released very quickly.
